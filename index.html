About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Kentucky, working in the Multimedia Information Analysis Laboratory (MIA Lab) under the supervision of Dr. Sen-ching Samson Cheung. My research focuses on data-efficient and cost-effective machine learning across across diverse modalities—spanning medical imaging, computational pathology, dermoscopy, and large-scale product data. I develop active learning, self-supervised, and foundation model-based approaches that reduce annotation cost while maintaining strong performance in challenging settings such as severe class imbalance, limited labeled data, and gigapixel-scale inputs.

Beyond academia, I have twice interned as an Applied Scientist at Amazon, shipping production-level multi-modal classification and generative AI systems, and spent three years as an Applications Engineer II at Synopsys validating industry-leading EDA verification tools. I hold a B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from the University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka (Washington Accord accredited).
